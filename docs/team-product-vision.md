## Organisation

### Where will you keep your Product & Sprint backlogs and any other documentation you are working on so that it is available to all team members?

GitHub Projects, with all documentation version-controlled in our GitHub repository.

### How will you update the task list in your Sprint backlog to show progress?

We will update task status in GitHub Projects during our weekly Tuesday meeting, moving cards through columns (To Do → In Progress → Done). For larger tasks, we may also update asynchronously throughout the week as work is individually completed.

### How and when will you prepare for the Sprint review meeting?

We will dedicate time at the end of each sprint (during our Tuesday meeting before the review) to prepare a demonstration of completed functionality using test data. We will review the product backlog, identify what to demo, and ensure the application is in a stable, demonstrable state.

## Product Vision

To transform urban landscapes into interactive, digital forests where every tree is known, named, and documented through an easy to use, mobile interface. We aim to empower our Tree Guardians with the digital tools necessary to document biodiversity and combat environmental threats through community action. Unlike similar tree observation platforms, adoption of trees and a gamified loyalty scheme help to keep contributors engaged and rewards caring for our canopy.
